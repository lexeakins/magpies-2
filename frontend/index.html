How Magpie Scores Website Matches

Magpie is trying to answer one question: does this website appear to belong to the same company in the source file? The score is an identity score, not an AI opinion, search rank, popularity score, or guarantee that the business is active.

1. Input

Start With The Source Row

Company name, city, state, and country are treated as the identity we are trying to match.

2. Candidates

Find Possible Websites

Google Maps always runs. Known URLs, Salesforce, legacy DB, and Perplexity can add candidate URLs when enabled.

3. Decision

Score Each URL

Each candidate URL is scored separately. The final URL is the strongest candidate that clears the run threshold.

Plain-English Scorecard

Company match

Compares the source company name to the Maps listing name or Perplexity official name. Exact or near-exact names are strong. Shared generic words are weaker.

Domain match

Compares the website domain to distinctive company words, acronyms, and initials. This supports the decision but is weaker than clear company and location evidence.

Location match

Looks for city and state evidence from Maps address data or search evidence. Same city and state helps. A different state is treated as a serious warning.

Caps and penalties

Weak name/domain evidence is capped. Contradicting locations are capped. Directory, social-profile, and provider-profile URLs are kept out of final results unless manually verified.

How To Read The Score

High means the deterministic identity verdict is accepted. This is usually a strong match, but review the evidence for important imports.

Medium means the deterministic identity verdict is review and the location does not contradict the source row.

Low means the candidate is weak, contradictory, or not promoted to a final URL. Treat it as unresolved unless a human confirms it.

No candidate is a valid outcome. A blank URL is better than inventing a wrong website.

What AI Does And Does Not Decide

Perplexity may help find candidate URLs and provide search evidence.

Known URLs are manually verified associations and remain auditable local evidence.

Salesforce and legacy DB can provide prior website evidence, but only as read-only candidate sources.

Haiku may inspect website text when enabled.

The final confidence score is calculated by Magpie from identity evidence. AI/provider confidence numbers should not be treated as ground truth.

Evidence Columns To Review

final_url is the selected website candidate.

final_confidence_score is 0 when no URL is selected.

url_source shows whether it came from Maps, Perplexity, Known URLs, Salesforce, or legacy DB.

company_match_score measures company-name alignment.

domain_match_score measures URL/domain alignment.

location_match_level summarizes city/state evidence.

identity_reason shows the short score explanation.

historical_identity_score scores Salesforce or legacy candidates.

historical_source shows whether prior evidence came from Salesforce or legacy DB.

manual_identity_score scores manually verified candidates.
